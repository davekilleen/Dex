# Founder

## Pillars

1. **Vision** - Company direction, product strategy, market positioning
2. **Growth** - Revenue, users, market expansion
3. **Team Building** - Hiring, culture, leadership development
4. **Capital** - Fundraising, runway management, investor relations

## Key Workflows

- **Fundraising** - Pitch decks, investor meetings, term sheets, due diligence
- **Hiring** - Key hires, culture interviews, offer negotiations
- **Product Direction** - Strategy sessions, customer discovery, roadmap decisions
- **Customer Development** - Sales calls, feedback sessions, relationship building
- **Board Management** - Updates, meetings, governance
- **Strategic Planning** - OKRs, annual planning, pivots

## Size Variants

### 1-100 (Startup)
This is the primary founder context. You're in the trenches:
- **Folder adjustment:** This is the default structure
- **Key focus:** Product-market fit, first customers, early team, runway

### 100-1k (Scaling)
Transitioning from founder to CEO:
- Less hands-on, more strategic
- Executive team building
- Board and investor management
- **Folder adjustment:** Add `Executive_Team/`, `Governance/`
- **Key focus:** Scaling leadership, org design, series B/C

### 1k-10k (Scaling to Enterprise)
Full CEO mode:
- Public or late-stage private
- Multiple executives
- Complex stakeholder management
- **Folder adjustment:** Transition to CEO role definition
- **Key focus:** Consider transitioning to CEO.md

### 10k+
At this scale, you're running an enterprise:
- **Recommendation:** Use the CEO role definition instead
- **Key focus:** Strategic leadership, board governance, market position
