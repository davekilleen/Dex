# Deerpath Leader James — Feedback Session

**Date:** 2026-03-12
**Participants:** James (Deerpath Capital), [[Jason_Wadler|Jason Wadler]], [[Jason_Whitney|Jason Whitney]], Regis Hadiaris

## Summary

Feedback session with James, a Deerpath Capital leader and friend of Jason Wadler. James provided sharp strategic input on The Wisory's positioning: the real value is the last 10-20% of insight that requires deep industry expertise — not the first 80% anyone can get from consultants or GLG. Emphasized quality of advisors, industry focus, and not competing on price. Walked through the mortgage case study and Wisery Score 2.0 (early stage screen + full diligence score).

## Key Discussion Points

- **Last 10% insight** is the differentiator — GLG is "a mile wide and an inch deep"; The Wisory needs to be "a mile deep"
- Best advisors don't want GLG anymore — burned out, data gets shared across clients
- Advisors want **privileged access to deals** (invest own money, board seats) not $600/hr
- **Industry focus matters:** Business services, healthcare, tech/software are where PE is active — NOT manufacturing/food & bev as previously assumed
- AI should be ~80% of focus right now given mindshare
- PE firms are not price sensitive — focus on value, not cost
- Risk of spreading too thin — quality bar on advisors must stay high
- Investment banker from Raymond James (Tech Services Group) interested in introductions
- Row Space ($50M Sequoia round) as potential partner or market signal

## Decisions

- Need to identify 3-4 target industries based on publicly available PE sector data
- Position as "the last 10%" — don't compete with cheap/cheerful consulting
- Don't try to tell PE firms they're wrong — provide considerations
- Wellness checks on existing portfolio companies is a strong use case
