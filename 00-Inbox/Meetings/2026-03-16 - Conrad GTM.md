# Conrad GTM

**Date:** 2026-03-16
**Participants:** Leo Jorge, Adam Rogove, Regis Hadiaris

## Summary

First working session for the Conrad 30-day GTM engagement. Leo and Adam walked Regis through the current product, target market, and challenges. Key pivot: focusing on wholesale mortgage brokers (medium-sized brokerages) rather than trying to hit everyone. Conrad's AI "Link" doesn't just convert leads — it pushes borrowers through the entire transaction workflow autonomously, from pre-qual through closing. UWM API integration enables a personalized home search portal that keeps leads away from Zillow/Redfin. Major adoption challenge: even at GRC (Adam's 80-LO shop), they can't get loan officers to sign up. NewRez meeting Thursday requires prep. Regis will run diagnostic scoring and deliver frameworks over 30 days.

## Key Discussion Points

- **Target customer crystallized:** Medium-sized wholesale mortgage brokerages (50+ LOs). TAM: ~50,000 LOs across ~12,000 brokers in wholesale. Not going after large companies (Guaranteed Rate, CrossCountry) who have their own systems. Not chasing small husband-and-wife shops.
- **The real differentiator (not just lead conversion):** Conrad breaks real estate transactions into workflow phases. Link AI pushes borrowers through each phase autonomously — searching, pre-qual, tours, loan application, closing. The loan officer is like a driver in a self-driving car — can jump in anytime but Link handles it. This is fundamentally different from "AI in a CRM."
- **UWM API integration:** Two critical APIs — pricing API (shows what borrower is approved for, creates personalized Zillow alternative) and full loan application import (AI collects everything and imports directly into UWM). Keeps leads from leaking to Zillow/Redfin.
- **Refi workflow:** When rates are appropriate to refi a client, Link AI reaches out automatically and handles the process. This is what GRC LOs are being pitched currently.
- **Adoption problem at GRC:** 80 LOs, struggling to get them signed up. Issues: premature early pitches, bugs during onboarding, LOs had bad taste from earlier referral-network version. Dan Sogorka's insight: adoption works when it's either mandatory (regulatory) or forced by the owner.
- **Conrad vs. Link naming:** Leo sees them as separate (Conrad = company/product, Link = AI like Siri is to iPhone). Regis pushed for simplicity — one name, one idea, one benefit. Withholding judgment until he knows more.
- **NewRez meeting Thursday:** Asking for API integration. Need to show why they benefit (AI funnels business to them). Leo doesn't have a deck yet — typically builds 2 days before. Adam wants something different than what they've been doing.
- **Competitive landscape:** West Cap (big broker) was receptive but has compliance concerns. C2, Nexus also in conversations but Regis recommended not chasing big ones yet.

## Decisions
