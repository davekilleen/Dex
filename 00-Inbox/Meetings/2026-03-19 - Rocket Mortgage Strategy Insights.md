## Key Discussion Points

- **Rocket Differentiation:** Three structural advantages — (1) 10+ petabytes of data, (2) heavy ML/AI investment over many years, (3) ownership of D2C mortgage channel. Banks have retreated; other independents lack brand/credibility/track record.
- **AI at Rocket:** Rocket Logic platform — conversational intelligence at scale, AI-powered document processing, agentic AI for task execution. Not theoretical — showing real ROI in productivity, turn times, conversion, quality. Heavily built on AWS with multiple models. Secret sauce is mortgage-specific agents moving from probabilistic to deterministic.
- **Purchase vs. Refi:** Fundamentally different businesses. Refi is transactional (working toward 4-day refi). Purchase is emotional, relationship-driven — real estate agent is quarterback, has scars/biases. Rocket leads D2C, UWM leads wholesale/broker.
- **Acquisitions:** Mr. Cooper expands servicing flywheel massively (not without risks). Redfin = buy-vs-build for early-stage purchase consumer data/eyeballs (Zillow-level scale hard to build from scratch). Integration risk is real.
- **Varun's Leadership:** Very smart, understood unit economics deeply, measured approach to getting up to speed. Comfortable in product/tech/data. Surrounded by mortgage experts for domain gaps. Right leader for multi-brand, multi-product scaling phase.
- **Competitive Landscape:** Rocket (D2C), UWM (wholesale), Penny Mac (correspondent) = big three. AI-native startups like Valon building from ground up in servicing. Wholesale broker channel consolidation happening (Arrive partnership).
- **Macro Risks:** Restricted inventory, rate lock-in effect, affordability crisis, baby boomers staying in homes longer. Internal risk: integrating three major acquisitions culturally.
