## Summary

Regis provided deep industry analysis to Ballast Research (public markets investment firm) on the mortgage origination space, drawing on his 21 years at Rocket Mortgage. The call covered Rocket's strategic direction under CEO Varun, the Redfin and Mr. Cooper acquisitions, UWM's broker channel dominance, AI's impact on origination costs, and untapped opportunities in home equity lending.

## Key Discussion Points

- **Rocket's strategic shift:** Bringing in Varun (external CEO from Intuit) signals a move toward M&A-driven growth — Redfin for purchase channel, Mr. Cooper for servicing flywheel
- **Three pillars for winning in mortgage:** Own a channel, have a servicing flywheel, find a path to reduce cost to originate
- **UWM vs. Rocket:** Both can claim leadership — UWM dominates wholesale/broker channel (sole focus on brokers), Rocket dominates direct-to-consumer. Different channels, both valid
- **Banks retreating:** Big banks (JPM, BofA) prefer playing in secondary market (providing capital) vs. front-end origination; consumer fintech has disintermediated cross-sell
- **Redfin acquisition:** Tricky — purchase market is hyper-local, homes selling before listing, unclear how portal traffic converts vs. local broker relationships
- **Mr. Cooper acquisition:** Regis would pick this over Redfin — "bird in the hand" — servicing clients are yours to lose
- **AI in mortgage:** Real opportunity to reduce manual touches, speed communication, reduce rework. Not replacing brokers but raising the bar. Agentic AI has high potential for loan manufacturing (underwriting, verification, compliance)
- **Human element persists:** Mortgages are scary, infrequent, confusing — consumers will always want human reassurance they're making the right decision
- **Home equity lending:** Massive untapped opportunity — trillions locked up because homeowners won't refi low pandemic-era rates. HELOCs, HELs, and substitute products could be bigger than expected
- **Cost to originate:** Hovering $8-15K forever; whoever materially reduces this wins because savings fund client acquisition dominance
- **Incumbent vs. FinTech race:** Can incumbents implement AI before AI-first startups scale? That's the key industry narrative
