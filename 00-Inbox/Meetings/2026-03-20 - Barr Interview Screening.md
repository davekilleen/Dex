## Summary

Initial phone screen for AI Consultant role at Barr Engineering. Barr is a ~1,150-employee, employee-owned engineering consulting firm headquartered in Minneapolis with 12 anchor offices (including Duluth). The role sits on a 7-person Digital Solutions team launched ~2024, focused on defining AI strategy and building client-facing AI consulting services. Flat organizational structure, no direct reports, hybrid work model. Regis expressed strong interest — culture, Duluth presence, and AI consulting focus align well.

## Key Discussion Points

- **Company Overview:** Founded 1960s by Doug Barr. ~1,150 employees. Employee-owned (ESOP). Growth through acquisitions of smaller firms (most recent: Seattle firm, Fall 2025). 12 anchor offices including 2 in Canada.
- **Role Details:** AI Consultant on Digital Solutions team (7 people). Define AI strategy, build service offerings, client-facing work. Not model-building — more strategy, use cases, service packaging. No direct reports (flat structure), informal mentorship.
- **Org Structure:** Very flat, no traditional "boss." Sponsors help new hires integrate. Free-market mentality — consultants choose work based on interest. High bar for hiring (selective panels).
- **Benefits Package:**
  - ESOP: Eligible after 1 year + 1,000 hours. First distribution in year 3. Averages ~5% of salary annually.
  - Year-end cash bonus: Individual + company performance. ~15-20% (15% more realistic for year 1).
  - Comp time plan: Hours over 40/week banked. At 120 hours, can pay out on paycheck or lump sum annually.
  - 4 weeks PTO at Regis's experience level.
  - $3,000 + 36 hours for individual education/training.
  - Health, dental, disability, life insurance from day 1.
  - 3% retirement contribution (not a match — straight contribution).
- **Interview Process:** Phone screen (done) -> Technical phone/video interview with Digital Solutions team -> Final panel interviews (2 panels, 3 people each, ~2.5-3 hours at Duluth office).
- **Compensation Discussion:** Regis acknowledged step down from SVP-level ($300K+) to senior IC. Top of posted range + bonus structure feels "doable." Marcy noted earning potential grows significantly over time.
